Interesting facts about Camels

  1. Their humps let them store up to 80 pounds of fat which they can live off for weeks and even months!
  2. When a camel finally does find water, he can drink up to 40 gallons in one go.
  3. Camels are very strong and can carry up to 900 pounds for 25 miles a day.

Interesting facts about Chameleons

  1. They mainly change colour in order to communicate or regulate body temperature.
  2. Unlike many lizards, chameleons can not regrow their tails.
  3. Their eyes can swivel around two different directions simultaneously.
  4. Smaller chameleons have faster tongues.

Interesting facts about Cicadas

  1. Most have red-orange eyes. But occasionally cicadas have blue, white or grayish eyes.
  2. Cicadas can survive a huge fall as babies, or nymphs.

Interesting facts about Pandas

  1. The giant panda's distinct black-and-white markings have two functions: camouflage and communication.
  2. Giant pandas spend 10-16 hours a day feeding, mainly on bamboo.

Interesting facts about Pigeons

  1. They can detect sounds at far lower frequencies than humans.
  2. Pigeons can fly at altitudes up to and beyond 6000 feet, and at an average speed of 77.6 mph.

Interesting facts about Teddy Bears

  1. Teddy bears got their name from the story that Teddy Roosevelt refused to shoot a bear cub while on a hunting trip in 1902.
  2. The world's smallest stitched teddy bear is a mere 0.29 inches tall!
  3. The world's largest stitched teddy bear is 55-foot!
